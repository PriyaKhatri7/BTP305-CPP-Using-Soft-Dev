Data Representation - IEEE 754
Single-Precision
Floating Point Numbers



To obtain the IEEE 754 representation of a single-precision floating point number: 

  • Set the sign bit to 0 if the number is positive, 1 if the number is negative. 
  • Determine the storable portion of the mantissa
    • Calculate the fractional binary of the absolute value of the number:
      • Find n the largest power of 2, where 2n divides into the number at least once
      • Subtract 2n from the number and retain the remainder
      • Reduce n by 1 and if the remainder is divisible by 2n, set the next bit to 1 and subtract 2n to obtain a new remainder.  If not divisible, set the next bit to 0
      • repeat the preceding step until either the remainder vanishes or there are no more significant bits left to fill.
    • convert the fractionary binary to exponent form using powers of 2 (for example, 1.0111011 * 2e) by shifting the decimal point immediately after the most significant bit
    • discard the most significant bit to obtain the storable portion of the mantissa
  • Determine the storable exponent
    • Add 127 to the exponent e
    • Convert the result to binary for the storable exponent
  • Assemble the sign bit, the storable exponent and the storable mantissa to form the complete binary

Example: 22.5

 Sign bit is 0
 Determine the storable portion of the mantissa:
  Determine the fractional binary:
   Find the largest power of 2 where 2 to that power divides into 22.5 at least once: 4
   16  1  22.5 - 16 = 6.5

    4  1  6.5 - 4 = 2.5

Determine the storable exponent:
 Add 127 to the exponent = 4 + 127 = 131
 In binary, 131 is 10000011
Assemble the components:
